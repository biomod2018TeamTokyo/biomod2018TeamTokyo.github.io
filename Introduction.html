Motivation and Background

Immune systems protect a human body. One of them is phagocytosis of macrophages [1]. Phagocytosis is a function that macrophages take in some foreign objects around them and deal with these objects. We have tried to imitate this system in microscale model using some DNA nanotechnologies: DNA hydrogels and strands. Our system will be able to be applied to gathering foreign objects or bacteria around one specific point.

DNA hydrogel is three-dimensional network structure made by DNA. It is easy to design structure with DNA strands. Therefore, DNA hydrogels have received considerable attention as one of structural DNA nanotechnologies. Previous study showed that, by adding DNA initiators, DNA hydrogels can be formed thorough self-assembly process, called Clamped Hybridization Chain Reaction (C-HCR) [2].

Our final goal is to achieve target collecting molecular-robot used as man-made immune system. However, considering the short experiment term in one summer project, we set a realistic goal.

Reference

[1] Alan Aderem & David M. Underhill (1999). MECHANISM OF PHAGOCYTOSIS IN MACROPHAGES. Annual Review of Immunology, 17, 593-623.
[2] Jianbang Wang, Jie Chao, Huajie Liu, Shao Su, Lianhui Wang, Wei Huang, Itamar Willner & Chunhai Fan (2017). Clamped Hybridization Chain Reactions for the Self-Assembly of Patterned DNA Hydrogels. Angewandte Chemie, 129(8), 2203-2207.

Our Goal and Project

To collect target substances, we took three steps: 1. Wrapping target, 2. Capturing the target and 3. Holding the target.

1. Wrapping target substances
In this experiment, we used DNA hydrogel as target substances. We designed H1, H2, I_H strand below. H1 and H2 have linker region (l in the picture below) to connect DNA arms. First, I_H binds to St1, which is the part of target gel (i’ in I_H and i in St1). I_H hybridizes H1 (a, b in H1 and a’, b’ in I_H). Then, H2 binds to H1 -I_H complex (c, b’ in H1 -I_H complex and c’, b in H2). Alternately, H1 and H2 hybridize this complex and finally DNA hydrogel are made. Through this self-assembly process, we covered the target with DNA hydrogel.

2. Capturing the hydrogel-covered target
DNA arms(made by C-HCR between A3 and A4) have linker parts connecting with DNA hydrogel (l in I_H-H1-H2 complex) and catch hydrogel-covered target.

3. Holding target by shrinking DNA arms
DNA arms consist of A1, A2 and C strand. Adding C’, C and C’ hybridize (z, x, x’ in C and z’, x’, x in C’), then A1 and A2 strands respectively make DNA hairpin structure. This process makes the DNA arms shrink.

Through Step1, 2 and 3, we aimed to catch target substances phagocytosis-like system.
